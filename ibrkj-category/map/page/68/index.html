In this article, we’ll take a closer look at the best ways to experience the rivers of New York in 2023.

    1. Get on a Boat

    One of the best ways to see the city from the water is to take a boat tour. There are numerous companies offering sightseeing cruises, dining experiences, and even party boats. Choose from a range of vessels, including tall ships, yachts, and speedboats, depending on your preferences and budget.

    2. Kayak the Hudson River

    For a more active adventure, consider renting a kayak and paddling along the Hudson River. There are several rental companies, such as Manhattan Kayak Company and Hudson River Expeditions, that offer guided tours or equipment rentals. You’ll get a unique perspective of the city skyline and the opportunity to explore some of the lesser-known areas of the river.

    3. Walk Along the East River Esplanade

    If you prefer to keep your feet on solid ground, take a stroll along the East River Esplanade. This pedestrian pathway runs from Battery Park to East Harlem and offers stunning views of Brooklyn, Queens, and the East River. You’ll also find parks, public art installations, and various other attractions along the way.

    4. Visit Governor’s Island

    Located just a short ferry ride from lower Manhattan, Governor’s Island is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. The island boasts several historic buildings, parks, and art installations, as well as spectacular views of the Statue of Liberty and the New York Harbor.

    5. Explore the Brooklyn Bridge Park

    Another great way to experience the rivers of New York is by visiting the Brooklyn Bridge Park. This 85-acre park offers a variety of activities, including sports fields, playgrounds, and picnic areas. You’ll also find several piers that offer incredible views of the Manhattan skyline and the East River.

    6. Take a Swim in the Hudson River

    Believe it or not, you can even swim in the Hudson River! The Floating Pool Lady, located on a barge in the river, offers a unique swimming experience with views of the city. There are also several designated swimming areas, such as the Hudson River Park Pier 26 Beach, where you can take a dip in the river.

    7. Enjoy a Picnic in Central Park

    While not directly located on the river, Central Park offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Pack a picnic and head to one of the park’s many scenic spots, such as the Great Lawn or Sheep Meadow, and enjoy views of the skyline and the nearby waterways.

    8. Attend River-Related Festivals and Events

    Throughout the year, there are several festivals and events that celebrate the rivers of New York. For example, the Clearwater Festival, held annually in June, features live music, environmental exhibits, and boat rides. The Hudson River Park’s Blues BBQ Festival, held in August, offers a day of food, music, and family-friendly activities.

    9. Volunteer for a River Cleanup

    If you’re passionate about preserving the environment, consider volunteering for a river cleanup event. Organizations such as Riverkeeper and the New York Harbor Foundation host cleanup events throughout the year, giving you the opportunity to make a difference while enjoying the beauty of the city’s waterways.

    10. Learn About the History of the Rivers

    Finally, take some time to learn about the rich history of the rivers of New York. The Museum of the City of New York offers several exhibits that explore the city’s relationship with its waterways, while the South Street Seaport Museum provides a glimpse into the city’s maritime past.

    Conclusion

    The rivers of New York offer a unique and often overlooked way to experience the city. Whether you prefer to explore by boat, kayak, or on foot, there are plenty of opportunities to see the city skyline and discover some of the lesser-known areas of the rivers. So, why not add a river adventure to your itinerary in 2023?
