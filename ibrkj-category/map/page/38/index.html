    Map Of Mexico States

    Posted on March 9, 2023 by admin

    Introduction

    Mexico is a beautiful country located in North America, known for its rich culture, history, and traditions. The country is divided into 31 states and 1 federal district, each with its own unique characteristics and attractions. In this article, we will explore the map of Mexico states and provide you with essential information about each state.

    1. Aguascalientes

    Aguascalientes is a small state located in the central part of Mexico. The state is known for its hot springs, beautiful parks, and historical sites. One of the most popular attractions in Aguascalientes is the San Marcos Fair, which takes place every year in April.

    2. Baja California

    Baja California is a state located in the northern part of Mexico, known for its beautiful beaches, seafood, and whale watching. The state is home to the famous tourist destination Cabo San Lucas, which is visited by millions of tourists every year.

    3. Baja California Sur

    Baja California Sur is another state located in the northern part of Mexico. The state is known for its beautiful beaches, desert landscapes, and outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and snorkeling. One of the most popular attractions in Baja California Sur is the Cabo Pulmo National Park, which is home to over 800 species of marine life.

    4. Campeche

    Campeche is a state located in the southeastern part of Mexico. The state is known for its beautiful colonial architecture, Mayan ruins, and natural attractions like waterfalls and cenotes. One of the most popular attractions in Campeche is the walled city of Campeche, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

    5. Chiapas

    Chiapas is a state located in the southern part of Mexico, known for its stunning natural landscapes, Mayan ruins, and cultural diversity. The state is home to the famous Palenque archaeological site, which is considered one of the most important Mayan ruins in Mexico.

    6. Chihuahua

    Chihuahua is a state located in the northern part of Mexico, known for its rugged terrain, deserts, and canyons. The state is home to the famous Copper Canyon, which is larger and deeper than the Grand Canyon in the United States.

    7. Coahuila

    Coahuila is a state located in the northeastern part of Mexico, known for its natural attractions like hot springs, mountains, and lakes. The state is also home to the famous Monclova meteorite, which is the largest meteorite ever found in Mexico.

    8. Colima

    Colima is a small state located in the western part of Mexico, known for its beautiful beaches, volcanoes, and archaeological sites. The state is home to the famous Colima volcano, which is one of the most active volcanoes in Mexico.

    9. Durango

    Durango is a state located in the northwestern part of Mexico, known for its rugged terrain, canyons, and deserts. The state is home to the famous Sierra Madre Occidental mountain range, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

    10. Guanajuato

    Guanajuato is a state located in the central part of Mexico, known for its colonial architecture, museums, and cultural events. The state is home to the famous city of Guanajuato, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

    Conclusion

    In conclusion, Mexico is a beautiful and diverse country with 31 states and 1 federal district, each with its own unique attractions and characteristics. Whether you are interested in exploring natural landscapes, historical sites, or cultural events, Mexico has something to offer for everyone. We hope this guide to the map of Mexico states has been helpful in planning your next trip to Mexico.

    Ponte Vecchio

    Ponte Vecchio is one of the most iconic landmarks in Florence. It is a medieval bridge that spans the Arno River and is lined with shops selling gold, jewelry, and souvenirs. The bridge connects the historic centre with the Oltrarno area.

    Oltrarno

    Oltrarno is a charming neighbourhood located on the other side of the Arno River. The area is known for its artisan workshops, antique shops, and small restaurants. It is a great place to explore if you want to get away from the crowds and experience the authentic side of Florence.

    Santa Croce

    Santa Croce is another popular area of Florence, located in the eastern part of the historic centre. It is home to the Basilica di Santa Croce, one of the most significant Franciscan churches in the world. The area is also famous for its leather shops and outdoor market, where you can find souvenirs and local products.

    Santo Spirito

    Santo Spirito is a lively neighbourhood located in the Oltrarno area. It is known for its vibrant nightlife, trendy bars, and restaurants. The area also has a beautiful church, the Basilica di Santo Spirito, which is worth a visit.

    San Lorenzo

    San Lorenzo is a busy area located in the northern part of the historic centre. It is home to the famous Mercato Centrale, a large indoor market selling fresh produce, meat, and fish. The area is also known for its leather shops, which offer a wide range of products at affordable prices.

    Conclusion

    Florence is a beautiful city with many areas to explore. Having a map of the city and its different neighbourhoods can help you plan your visit and make the most of your time.
