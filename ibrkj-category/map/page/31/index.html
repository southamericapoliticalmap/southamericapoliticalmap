    The Union Territories of India

    The eight union territories of India are Andaman and Nicobar Islands, Chandigarh, Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu, Lakshadweep, Delhi, Puducherry, Jammu and Kashmir, and Ladakh. Each of these territories has its own distinct features that make them unique.

    Chandigarh

    Chandigarh is a city and union territory that serves as the capital of the two states of Punjab and Haryana. It is known for its urban planning and architecture. The city is divided into sectors, each with its own unique features.

    Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu

    Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu were merged into one union territory in 2020. The territory is known for its scenic beauty and is a popular tourist destination. The capital city of this union territory is Daman.

    Lakshadweep

    Lakshadweep is a group of islands located in the Arabian Sea. It is known for its beautiful beaches, coral reefs, and lagoons. The capital city of Lakshadweep is Kavaratti.

    Delhi

    Delhi is the capital city of India and is also a union territory. It is known for its historical monuments, bustling markets, and diverse culture. The city is divided into two parts, Old Delhi and New Delhi.

    Puducherry

    Puducherry, also known as Pondicherry, is a former French colony located in South India. It is known for its French architecture, beaches, and spiritual centers. The capital city of Puducherry is Pondicherry.

    Jammu and Kashmir

    Jammu and Kashmir is a union territory located in the northernmost part of India. It is known for its scenic beauty, mountains, and valleys. The capital city of Jammu and Kashmir is Srinagar.

    Ladakh

    Ladakh is a union territory located in the northernmost part of India. It is known for its rugged terrain, mountains, and Buddhist culture. The capital city of Ladakh is Leh.
