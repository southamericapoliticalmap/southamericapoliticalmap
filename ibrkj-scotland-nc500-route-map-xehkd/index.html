    Scotland Nc500 Route Map

    Posted on March 8, 2023 by admin

    Introduction

    Scotland’s North Coast 500 (NC500) route map is a 516-mile road trip that circles the northernmost part of Scotland. It takes you through some of the most beautiful and remote landscapes in the country, including rugged coastlines, snow-capped mountains, and quaint villages. In this article, we’ll explore some tips and tricks for driving the NC500 and highlight some must-see sights along the way.

    Getting Started

    Before you hit the road, make sure you have a detailed map of the NC500 route. You can download a free map from the official NC500 website or pick up a paper map at one of the tourist information centers along the route. It’s also a good idea to familiarize yourself with Scotland’s driving laws and regulations, such as driving on the left side of the road and using roundabouts.

    Where to Start

    The NC500 can be driven in either direction, but most people start in Inverness and travel clockwise around the route. Inverness is the largest city in the Scottish Highlands and has plenty of accommodation options, restaurants, and attractions to explore before you begin your journey.

    Top Sights Along the Way

    As you travel along the NC500, you’ll encounter numerous stunning sights and stops. Some of the must-see attractions include the fairytale-like Dunrobin Castle, the breathtakingly beautiful Loch Maree, and the iconic John o’ Groats signpost, which marks the northernmost point of mainland Britain.

    When to Go

    The NC500 route is open year-round, but the best time to visit is between April and October when the weather is milder and the days are longer. Keep in mind that the summer months can be busy, so book your accommodation in advance to avoid disappointment.

    Where to Stay

    There are plenty of accommodation options along the NC500, from cozy B&Bs to luxurious hotels. Consider staying in one of the charming coastal villages, such as Ullapool or Dornoch, for an authentic Scottish experience. You can also opt to camp at one of the many campsites along the route.

    What to Pack

    When packing for your NC500 road trip, make sure to bring warm and waterproof clothing, as the weather can be unpredictable. Comfortable walking shoes are also a must, as there are plenty of hiking trails and scenic walks to explore. Don’t forget to pack your camera to capture the stunning scenery.

    Food and Drink

    Scotland is known for its delicious cuisine, and the NC500 route is no exception. Make sure to try some classic Scottish dishes, such as haggis, neeps, and tatties, and indulge in some fresh seafood, including prawns, scallops, and smoked salmon. Don’t forget to try some of Scotland’s famous whiskey and craft beer, too.

    Driving Tips

    Driving along the NC500 can be challenging, especially if you’re not used to driving on narrow, winding roads. Take your time and drive carefully, especially when passing other vehicles or encountering livestock on the road. Be sure to fill up your gas tank whenever you see a petrol station, as they can be few and far between.

    Conclusion

    Scotland’s NC500 route map is a scenic and unforgettable road trip that takes you through some of the most stunning landscapes in the country. With these tips and tricks, you’ll be well-prepared to explore the route and make the most of your Scottish adventure. So pack your bags, hit the road, and get ready for an unforgettable journey.
