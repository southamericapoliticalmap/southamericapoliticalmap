    Introduction

    JFK Airport, also known as John F. Kennedy International Airport, is one of the largest and busiest airports in the world. It is located in the borough of Queens in New York City, and it serves as a gateway to the United States for millions of travelers every year. In this article, we will provide you with a complete guide to the JFK Airport New York map, including its terminals, transportation options, and tips for navigating this massive airport.

    Terminal Map

    JFK Airport has six terminals, each with its own set of gates, ticket counters, and baggage claim areas. Terminal 1 is mainly used by international carriers, while Terminal 2 is used by Delta Air Lines. Terminal 3 is currently closed, and its gates have been integrated into other terminals. Terminal 4 is the main terminal at JFK, and it is used by many international airlines, including Emirates and Delta. Terminal 5 is used by JetBlue Airways, while Terminal 6 is used by JetBlue and Alaska Airlines.

    Terminal 1

    Terminal 1 has 11 gates and serves many international airlines, including Air France, Japan Airlines, and Lufthansa. The terminal has a variety of dining and shopping options, including duty-free shops, restaurants, and cafes.

    Terminal 2

    Terminal 2 has 11 gates and is used exclusively by Delta Air Lines. The terminal has a Sky Club lounge for Delta’s premium passengers, as well as a variety of dining and shopping options.

    Terminal 4

    Terminal 4 is the main terminal at JFK, with 38 gates and a wide variety of dining and shopping options. The terminal is used by many international airlines, including Emirates, Delta, and KLM. It also has a duty-free shopping area, as well as a Priority Pass lounge for eligible passengers.

    Terminal 5

    Terminal 5 is used exclusively by JetBlue Airways and has 29 gates. The terminal has a variety of dining and shopping options, including a Shake Shack restaurant and a duty-free shop.

    Terminal 6

    Terminal 6 is used by JetBlue Airways and Alaska Airlines, with 15 gates in total. The terminal has a variety of dining and shopping options, including a Starbucks coffee shop and a duty-free shop.

    Transportation Options

    JFK Airport is located approximately 15 miles (24 kilometers) southeast of Manhattan, and there are several transportation options available for travelers. The AirTrain JFK is a rail system that connects all of the airport’s terminals and provides connections to the New York City subway system and Long Island Rail Road. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available, as well as private car services and rental cars.

    Tips for Navigating JFK Airport

    Navigating JFK Airport can be overwhelming, but there are several tips that can help make your experience smoother. First, be sure to check your terminal and gate information before arriving at the airport, as this can change frequently. Second, give yourself plenty of time to navigate the airport, especially if you are traveling during peak travel times. Finally, take advantage of the airport’s dining and shopping options, as there are many great choices available throughout the airport.

    Conclusion

    JFK Airport can be a daunting place to navigate, but with the right information and preparation, you can have a smooth and stress-free travel experience. Use this guide to the JFK Airport New York map to plan your next trip and make the most of your time at this iconic airport.
