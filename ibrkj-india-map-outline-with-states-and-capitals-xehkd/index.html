    Introduction

    India, the seventh-largest country by area, is a land of diversity with 28 states and 8 Union territories. The country has a rich culture, history, and geography that makes it an interesting place for exploration. If you are planning to visit India or want to learn more about it, understanding the India map outline with states and capitals is crucial. In this article, we will discuss the India map outline, its states, and capitals.

    India Map Outline

    The India map outline shows the country’s shape, which is a subcontinent located in South Asia. The map has four distinct regions, including the Northern Mountains, Indo-Gangetic Plain, Deccan Plateau, and Coastal Plains. The Northern Mountains are home to the Himalayas, the highest mountain range globally, while the Indo-Gangetic Plain is primarily an agricultural region. The Deccan Plateau is a highland region with a diverse landscape, and the Coastal Plains are narrow strips of land along the Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal.
