    What are Zip Codes?

    Zip codes are a system of postal codes used by the United States Postal Service (USPS) since 1963. They were introduced to make the sorting and delivery of mail more efficient. Zip codes are made up of five digits and are assigned to specific geographic areas, such as cities, towns, and neighborhoods. Each zip code represents a unique area, and it makes it easier for USPS to sort and deliver mail to the correct addresses.
