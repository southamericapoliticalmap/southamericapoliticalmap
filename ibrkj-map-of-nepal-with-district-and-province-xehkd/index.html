    Overview of Nepal’s Administrative Divisions

    Nepal is a small yet diverse country located in South Asia, nestled between India and China. It is divided into seven provinces, which were established in 2015, and seventy-seven districts. Each province is further divided into districts, which are the administrative units responsible for local governance. The provinces and districts each have their own unique culture, customs, and traditions, making Nepal a fascinating destination to explore.

    The Seven Provinces of Nepal

    The seven provinces of Nepal are named after the major rivers that flow through them. They are as follows: 1. Province No. 1: This province is located in the eastern part of Nepal and includes the districts of Taplejung, Panchthar, Ilam, Jhapa, Morang, Sunsari, Dhankuta, Terhathum, Sankhuwasabha, and Bhojpur. 2. Province No. 2: This province is located in the southern part of Nepal and includes the districts of Saptari, Siraha, Dhanusha, Mahottari, Sarlahi, Rautahat, and Bara. 3. Province No. 3: This province is located in the central part of Nepal and includes the districts of Sindhupalchowk, Rasuwa, Dhading, Nuwakot, Kathmandu, Lalitpur, Bhaktapur, Kavrepalanchowk, and Chitwan. 4. Province No. 4: This province is located in the western part of Nepal and includes the districts of Baglung, Parbat, Myagdi, Mustang, Manang, and Lamjung. 5. Province No. 5: This province is located in the mid-western part of Nepal and includes the districts of Rukum, Rolpa, Pyuthan, Arghakhanchi, Gulmi, Palpa, Nawalparasi, Banke, Bardiya, and Dang. 6. Province No. 6: This province is located in the far-western part of Nepal and includes the districts of Humla, Mugu, Dolpa, Jumla, Kalikot, Jajarkot, and Dailekh. 7. Province No. 7: This province is located in the western part of Nepal and includes the districts of Baitadi, Dadeldhura, Darchula, Bajhang, Bajura, Achham, and Doti.

    The Seventy-Seven Districts of Nepal

    Each of Nepal’s seventy-seven districts has its own history, culture, and geography. Some of the most popular districts for tourists include: 1. Kathmandu: The capital of Nepal and a hub for tourism and business. 2. Pokhara: A popular destination for adventure tourism, known for its stunning scenery and outdoor activities. 3. Chitwan: Home to Chitwan National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the best places in Nepal to see wildlife. 4. Mustang: A remote district in the Himalayas, known for its stunning scenery and traditional Tibetan culture. 5. Bhaktapur: A historic city with well-preserved architecture and culture, located just outside of Kathmandu.

    Exploring Nepal’s Map

    Nepal’s map is a fascinating tool for exploring the country’s diverse geography and culture. Whether you’re interested in trekking in the Himalayas, learning about the culture of the Kathmandu Valley, or exploring the wildlife of Chitwan National Park, Nepal’s map can help you plan your trip and discover new destinations. With its rich history and diverse culture, Nepal is a country that is sure to leave a lasting impression on anyone who visits.
