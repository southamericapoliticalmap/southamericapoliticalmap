    History of the Jewish Ghetto in Venice

    The Jewish Ghetto in Venice was established in the early 16th century, and it is one of the oldest ghettos in the world. The Jewish population was forced to live in this area, which was surrounded by canals and bridges. During this time, the Jewish community in Venice faced persecution, and they were not allowed to leave the ghetto during the night.

    The Map of the Jewish Ghetto in Venice

    The Jewish Ghetto in Venice is located in the Cannaregio district and is surrounded by canals on three sides. The area is divided into two sections, the Ghetto Vecchio and the Ghetto Nuovo. The Ghetto Vecchio is the oldest part of the ghetto and is home to five synagogues. The Ghetto Nuovo was established in the 16th century and is home to three synagogues.

    Places to Visit in the Jewish Ghetto

    One of the most popular places to visit in the Jewish Ghetto is the Jewish Museum of Venice. This museum is located in the Campo del Ghetto Nuovo and contains a collection of artifacts and documents that tell the story of the Jewish community in Venice. Another must-visit place is the Scuola Grande Tedesca, which is a beautiful building that was once used as a hospital for the Jewish community.

    Food in the Jewish Ghetto

    One of the best things about visiting the Jewish Ghetto in Venice is the food.     Events in the Jewish Ghetto

    Throughout the year, there are many events that take place in the Jewish Ghetto. One of the most popular events is the Jewish Film Festival, which takes place in November. This festival showcases Jewish films from around the world and attracts visitors from all over.
