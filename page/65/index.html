    The northern states of Mexico are known for their rugged natural beauty and rich history.     The central region of Mexico is the heart of the country and is home to the capital city, Mexico City. This region is known for its rich history, colonial architecture, and delicious cuisine.     Visiting Morelos

    Morelos is a small state located south of Mexico City. This state is known for its beautiful colonial architecture and the stunning Tepozteco Mountain. The city of Cuernavaca, also known as the “City of Eternal Spring,” is a popular destination for tourists.

    Exploring Southern Mexico

    The southern region of Mexico is known for its tropical climate, beautiful beaches, and ancient ruins. Oaxaca, one of the states in this region, is famous for its vibrant culture, delicious cuisine, and the stunning Monte Alban ruins. Chiapas, on the other hand, is home to the majestic Sumidero Canyon and the mystical city of San Cristobal de las Casas.

    Discovering Guerrero

    Guerrero is a state located on the Pacific coast of Mexico. This state is known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and the famous cliff divers of Acapulco. The city of Taxco, also located in Guerrero, is famous for its silver mines and the stunning Santa Prisca Church.

    Conclusion

    The Basics of Time Zones

    Time zones are geographical regions that follow a uniform standard time throughout. The world is divided into 24 time zones, each separated by one hour from its neighboring zone.     Central Time Zone

    The central time zone covers the central part of the Gulf of Mexico, including cities such as Houston, Dallas, and San Antonio. This time zone is six hours behind Coordinated Universal Time (UTC-6).

    Mountain Time Zone

    The mountain time zone covers the western part of the Gulf of Mexico, including cities such as El Paso and Albuquerque. This time zone is seven hours behind Coordinated Universal Time (UTC-7).

    Pacific Time Zone

    The Pacific time zone covers the far western part of the Gulf of Mexico, including cities such as Los Angeles and San Francisco. This time zone is eight hours behind Coordinated Universal Time (UTC-8).

    Daylight Saving Time

    Daylight saving time (DST) is a practice of advancing the clock by one hour during the summer months to extend the daylight hours in the evening. Some states in the Gulf of Mexico region, such as Florida, observe DST, while others, such as Arizona, do not.
