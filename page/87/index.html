    Exploring Central Park

    The Empire State Building

    For a bird’s eye view of the city, head to the top of the Empire State Building. This iconic skyscraper stands at 1,454 feet tall and provides stunning 360-degree views of the Manhattan skyline. Don’t forget to snap a selfie on the observation deck!

    The Statue of Liberty

    A symbol of freedom and democracy, the Statue of Liberty is a must-see attraction for any visitor to New York City. Take a ferry ride to Liberty Island and climb to the top of the statue’s pedestal for breathtaking views of the harbor.

    The Metropolitan Museum of Art

    Art aficionados will appreciate the extensive collection at the Metropolitan Museum of Art. This world-renowned museum boasts over two million works of art, from ancient artifacts to contemporary masterpieces. Don’t miss the Egyptian Temple of Dendur and the European Paintings galleries.

    Times Square

    The crossroads of the world, Times Square is a neon-lit hub of activity day and night. Take in the lights and sounds of Broadway, grab a bite to eat at one of the many restaurants, or catch a show at one of the theaters.

    The High Line

    For a unique perspective on the city, take a walk on the High Line. This elevated park is built on an abandoned railroad track and offers stunning views of the Hudson River and the city skyline. Stop for a snack at one of the food vendors or take a break on one of the many benches.

    Brooklyn Bridge

    Take a stroll across the iconic Brooklyn Bridge for breathtaking views of the Manhattan skyline. This historic suspension bridge spans the East River and is a popular spot for photographers and tourists alike.

    The 9/11 Memorial and Museum

    Pay your respects at the 9/11 Memorial and Museum, a poignant reminder of the tragic events of September 11th, 2001. The museum features exhibits and artifacts honoring the victims and heroes of that fateful day.

    Coney Island

    Escape the city heat with a trip to Coney Island. This classic boardwalk is home to amusement park rides, carnival games, and plenty of tasty treats. Don’t miss a ride on the historic Cyclone roller coaster or a hot dog from Nathan’s Famous.

    The Bronx Zoo

    Animal lovers will enjoy a visit to the Bronx Zoo, home to over 6,000 animals from around the world. Explore the African Plains exhibit or take a trip to the Butterfly Garden. Don’t miss the daily sea lion show or the bird of prey exhibit. With this ultimate map guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to New York City. Be sure to visit these top attractions and discover all that the Big Apple has to offer.
