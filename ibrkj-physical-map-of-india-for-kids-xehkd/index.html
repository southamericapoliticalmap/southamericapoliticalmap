    Physical Map Of India For Kids

    Posted on October 9, 2022 by admin

    Introduction

    India is a vast country with diverse landscapes and terrains. From the towering Himalayas in the north to the sun-kissed beaches in the south, India has it all. Understanding India’s physical map is essential for kids to grasp the country’s geography and appreciate its natural beauty. In this article, we will take a closer look at India’s physical map and explore its various features.

    What is a Physical Map?

    A physical map is a representation of the Earth’s surface that shows the physical features of a region, such as mountains, rivers, lakes, and oceans. Physical maps help us understand the natural landscape of a place and the features that make it unique.

    India’s Physical Map

    India’s physical map is characterized by its diverse terrain. The country has three main geographical regions – the Himalayas in the north, the Indo-Gangetic Plain in the middle, and the Peninsular Plateau in the south. The Himalayas are the world’s highest mountain range, while the Indo-Gangetic Plain is one of the world’s most fertile regions. The Peninsular Plateau is a vast elevated region that covers most of southern India.

    Himalayas

    The Himalayas are the most prominent feature of India’s physical map. The range stretches for over 2,400 km and is home to some of the world’s highest peaks, including Mount Everest. The Himalayas are also the source of several major rivers in India, such as the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Indus.

    Indo-Gangetic Plain

    The Indo-Gangetic Plain is a vast alluvial plain that stretches from the Himalayas to the Deccan Plateau. The plain is formed by the deposition of sediments by the rivers that flow through it. The region is one of the most fertile in the world and is home to some of India’s largest cities, such as Delhi, Kolkata, and Lucknow.

    Peninsular Plateau

    The Peninsular Plateau is a vast elevated region that covers most of southern India. The plateau is characterized by its rugged terrain and rocky outcrops. The region is home to several important rivers, such as the Godavari, Krishna, and Cauvery. The Western Ghats and the Eastern Ghats are two mountain ranges that run along the edges of the plateau.

    Conclusion

    India’s physical map is a fascinating subject to explore for kids. Understanding the country’s diverse terrain and natural features can help them appreciate the beauty of the Indian subcontinent. We hope this article has provided you with some valuable insights into India’s physical map and its various features.
