    List Of Ny Counties A-Z

    Posted on April 23, 2023 by admin

    Introduction

    New York, the Empire State, is the fourth most populous state in the United States. It is home to more than 19 million people, and is known for its beautiful landscapes, rich culture, and bustling cities. The state is divided into 62 counties, each with its unique character and charm. In this article, we will provide a comprehensive list of NY counties from A to Z.

    A

    Albany County

    Located in the eastern part of the state, Albany County is the capital of New York. It is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and beautiful landscapes. The county is home to more than 300,000 residents and covers an area of 533 square miles.

    Allegany County

    Allegany County is located in the western part of the state and is known for its scenic beauty and outdoor activities. The county covers an area of 1,034 square miles and is home to more than 45,000 residents.

    B

    Broome County

    Broome County is located in the southern part of the state and is known for its diverse economy, cultural attractions, and natural beauty. The county covers an area of 716 square miles and is home to more than 190,000 residents.

    Bronx County

    Bronx County is located in the southern part of the state and is one of the five boroughs of New York City. It is known for its vibrant culture, historic landmarks, and world-renowned attractions. The county covers an area of 57 square miles and is home to more than 1.4 million residents.

    C

    Cattaraugus County

    Cattaraugus County is located in the western part of the state and is known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. The county covers an area of 1,310 square miles and is home to more than 76,000 residents.

    Cayuga County

    Cayuga County is located in the central part of the state and is known for its scenic beauty, historic landmarks, and cultural attractions. The county covers an area of 864 square miles and is home to more than 78,000 residents.

    D

    Delaware County

    Delaware County is located in the eastern part of the state and is known for its rural beauty, outdoor activities, and historic landmarks. The county covers an area of 1,468 square miles and is home to more than 44,000 residents.

    Dutchess County

    Dutchess County is located in the eastern part of the state and is known for its scenic beauty, historic landmarks, and vibrant culture. The county covers an area of 825 square miles and is home to more than 294,000 residents.

    E

    Erie County

    Erie County is located in the western part of the state and is known for its diverse economy, cultural attractions, and natural beauty. The county covers an area of 1,227 square miles and is home to more than 918,000 residents.

    Essex County

    Essex County is located in the northeastern part of the state and is known for its scenic beauty, outdoor activities, and historic landmarks. The county covers an area of 1,795 square miles and is home to more than 36,000 residents.

    F

    Franklin County

    Franklin County is located in the northeastern part of the state and is known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and historic landmarks. The county covers an area of 1,697 square miles and is home to more than 50,000 residents.

    Fulton County

    Fulton County is located in the central part of the state and is known for its scenic beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. The county covers an area of 533 square miles and is home to more than 53,000 residents.

    G

    Genesee County

    Genesee County is located in the western part of the state and is known for its agricultural landscapes, historic landmarks, and cultural attractions. The county covers an area of 495 square miles and is home to more than 57,000 residents.

    Greene County

    Greene County is located in the eastern part of the state and is known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and historic landmarks. The county covers an area of 658 square miles and is home to more than 47,000 residents.

    H

    Hamilton County

    Hamilton County is located in the northeastern part of the state and is known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities, and historic landmarks. The county covers an area of 1,717 square miles and is home to more than 4,000 residents.

    Herkimer County

    Herkimer County is located in the central part of the state and is known for its scenic beauty, outdoor activities, and historic landmarks. The county covers an area of 1,458 square miles and is home to more than 61,000 residents.

    Conclusion

    New York is a state full of diversity, beauty, and culture. Its 62 counties each have their unique character and charm, offering something for everyone.
