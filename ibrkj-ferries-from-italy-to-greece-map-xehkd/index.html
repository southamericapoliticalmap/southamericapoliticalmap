    Ferries From Italy To Greece Map

    Posted on February 27, 2023 by admin

    Introduction

    If you’re planning a trip from Italy to Greece, taking a ferry is one of the most convenient and cost-effective ways to travel. With numerous ports available at both ends of the journey, it’s important to know the different routes and options available to you. In this article, we’ll provide a comprehensive guide to help you plan your trip and navigate the ferry system.

    What Are the Main Ferry Routes?

    There are several ferry routes between Italy and Greece, with the most popular ones being from Bari, Ancona, and Venice to Igoumenitsa and Patras. These routes are operated by several ferry companies, including Minoan Lines, Anek Lines, Superfast Ferries, and Blue Star Ferries. Depending on the route, the journey can take anywhere from 8 to 24 hours.

    Bari to Igoumenitsa

    If you’re traveling from southern Italy, the Bari to Igoumenitsa route is the most convenient option. This route is operated by several ferry companies, including Ventouris Ferries, Superfast Ferries, and Anek Lines. The journey takes approximately 8-9 hours, and there are usually 2-3 sailings per week.

    Ancona to Patras

    The Ancona to Patras route is the most popular one, with daily sailings during the summer season. This route is operated by several ferry companies, including Minoan Lines, Anek Lines, and Superfast Ferries. The journey takes approximately 24 hours, and there are usually several departures per week during the off-peak season.

    Venice to Patras

    The Venice to Patras route is operated by Anek Lines, with several departures per week during the summer season. The journey takes approximately 26 hours, and the ferry usually stops at Igoumenitsa before reaching its final destination.

    How to Book a Ferry

    Booking a ferry from Italy to Greece is easy, and can be done online or at the port. It’s recommended to book your ferry in advance, especially during the peak season, to avoid any disappointment. You can book your ferry ticket directly with the ferry company or through a travel agent. Make sure to check the ferry timetable and schedule before booking to ensure that it fits your travel plans.

    What Are the Ferry Prices?

    Ferry prices vary depending on the route, the ferry company, and the time of year. Generally, the prices are higher during the peak season (June to August) and lower during the off-peak season (September to May). A one-way ticket from Bari to Igoumenitsa can cost anywhere from €40 to €100, while a one-way ticket from Ancona to Patras can cost between €50 and €150.

    What to Expect on Board

    Ferry companies operating between Italy and Greece offer a range of onboard facilities and services, including restaurants, cafes, bars, shops, and entertainment. Depending on the ferry, you may have the option of booking a cabin or a seat in the economy class. Most ferries also offer free Wi-Fi and charging points.

    What to Bring on Board

    When traveling on a ferry, it’s important to bring all the necessary documents, including your passport or ID card. You should also pack a small bag with essentials such as a change of clothes, toiletries, and any medications you may need. It’s recommended to bring a book or some entertainment to keep you occupied during the journey.

    Tips for a Smooth Journey

    To ensure a smooth journey, it’s recommended to arrive at the port at least 1-2 hours before the scheduled departure time. This will give you enough time to check-in, go through security, and board the ferry. Make sure to also check the weather forecast before traveling and pack accordingly.

    Conclusion

    Taking a ferry from Italy to Greece can be an enjoyable and cost-effective way to travel. With several routes and options available, it’s important to plan your journey in advance to ensure a smooth and hassle-free experience. By following the tips and guidelines provided in this article, you can make the most of your ferry trip and enjoy all that Italy and Greece have to offer.
