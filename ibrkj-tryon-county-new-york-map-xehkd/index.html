    The History of Tryon County

    Tryon County was established in 1772, and it covered a vast area of western New York, including parts of the Adirondack Mountains and the Mohawk Valley. It was named after William Tryon, the governor of New York at the time. However, the county’s short-lived existence was marred by conflicts between loyalists and patriots during the American Revolution. In 1784, the state of New York abolished Tryon County and divided its territory into several new counties.
