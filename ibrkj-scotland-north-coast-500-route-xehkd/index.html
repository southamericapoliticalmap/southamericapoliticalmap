    Scotland North Coast 500 Route

    Posted on November 30, 2022 by admin

    Introduction

    Scotland is famous for its breathtaking scenery and rich cultural heritage. The North Coast 500 route is a 516-mile journey that showcases the best of Scotland’s natural beauty. The route takes you through some of Scotland’s most stunning landscapes, from rugged coastlines to majestic mountains. In this article, we’ll explore the North Coast 500 route and discover why it’s quickly becoming one of the most popular tourist destinations in Scotland.

    History of the North Coast 500 Route

    The North Coast 500 route was launched in 2015 as a way to promote tourism in Scotland’s northern region. The route starts and ends in Inverness, taking you through some of the most remote and unspoiled areas of Scotland. The route has quickly gained popularity among tourists from all over the world, and it’s not hard to see why.

    The Route

    The North Coast 500 route takes you through some of Scotland’s most stunning landscapes. The route starts in Inverness and heads north to the coastal town of Ullapool. From there, you’ll travel along the rugged coastline to the village of Durness. The route then heads eastward to John o’ Groats, the northernmost point of mainland Scotland. Finally, you’ll head south back to Inverness.

    What to See and Do

    The North Coast 500 route offers a wide range of activities for visitors. From hiking and cycling to kayaking and fishing, there’s something for everyone. Some of the highlights include the stunning beaches of Durness, the majestic mountains of Assynt, and the historic village of Tongue. Along the way, you’ll also find plenty of quaint villages, charming cafes, and local pubs.

    Accommodations

    There are plenty of accommodations along the North Coast 500 route, from luxury hotels to cozy bed and breakfasts. Many of these accommodations offer stunning views of the surrounding landscapes, and some even offer guided tours of the local area. If you’re looking for a unique experience, you can also choose to stay in a traditional Scottish castle or a remote cabin in the wilderness.

    Tips for Traveling the North Coast 500 Route

    If you’re planning a trip along the North Coast 500 route, here are some tips to make your journey even more enjoyable:

    Plan Ahead

    The North Coast 500 route is a popular destination, especially during the summer months. It’s important to plan ahead and book your accommodations and activities in advance to avoid disappointment.

    Bring Appropriate Clothing

    Scotland’s weather can be unpredictable, so it’s important to bring appropriate clothing for all types of weather. Make sure to pack a rain jacket and warm layers, even if you’re traveling during the summer months.

    Drive with Caution

    The North Coast 500 route can be challenging for even experienced drivers. The roads are narrow and winding, and you may encounter livestock or other hazards on the road. Make sure to drive with caution and take your time to enjoy the scenery.

    Conclusion

    The North Coast 500 route is a journey that showcases the best of Scotland’s natural beauty. From rugged coastlines to majestic mountains, this route offers something for everyone. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or just looking for a relaxing getaway, the North Coast 500 route is a destination worth exploring. So pack your bags, hit the road, and discover the beauty of Scotland’s North Coast 500 route.
