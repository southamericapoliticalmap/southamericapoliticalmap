    Introduction

    Are you planning a trip to New Mexico and wondering what places you should visit? One city that should definitely be on your list is Roswell. This city is known for its connection to extraterrestrial life and has a lot to offer visitors. In this article, we’ll explore the best places to visit in Roswell using a New Mexico map.

    1. International UFO Museum and Research Center

    The International UFO Museum and Research Center is a must-visit place for anyone interested in the Roswell Incident. This museum has a vast collection of exhibits related to the incident, including documents, pictures, and videos. Visitors can also attend lectures and presentations on the subject.

    2. Bitter Lake National Wildlife Refuge

    If you’re a nature lover, then the Bitter Lake National Wildlife Refuge is a must-visit place. This refuge has over 20,000 acres of wetlands, grasslands, and desert landscapes. Visitors can hike, bird-watch, and observe wildlife in their natural habitat.

    3. Roswell Museum and Art Center

    The Roswell Museum and Art Center is another must-visit place in Roswell. This museum has a vast collection of art, including works by Georgia O’Keeffe, Peter Hurd, and Henriette Wyeth. Visitors can also explore exhibits related to the history and culture of the Southwest.

    4. Bottomless Lakes State Park

    Another natural wonder in Roswell is the Bottomless Lakes State Park. This park has nine deep, blue lakes that were formed by sinkholes. Visitors can swim, fish, and hike in this beautiful park.

    5. Anderson Museum of Contemporary Art

    The Anderson Museum of Contemporary Art is a must-visit place for art lovers. This museum has a vast collection of contemporary art, including works by local and international artists. Visitors can also attend exhibitions, workshops, and events.

    6. Alien Zone

    For those interested in extraterrestrial life, the Alien Zone is a fun place to visit. This store has a vast collection of UFO-related merchandise, including t-shirts, toys, and books. Visitors can also take pictures with life-size aliens and replicas of the Roswell Incident.

    7. Spring River Park and Zoo

    The Spring River Park and Zoo is a fun place to visit with kids. This zoo has over 300 animals, including tigers, bears, and monkeys. Visitors can also ride a train and explore a farm exhibit.

    8. Historical Center for Southeast New Mexico

    The Historical Center for Southeast New Mexico is a must-visit place for history buffs. This center has a vast collection of artifacts related to the history of Southeast New Mexico, including exhibits on ranching, oil, and military history. Visitors can also attend lectures and events.

    9. Cahoon Park

    Cahoon Park is a beautiful park in Roswell that’s perfect for picnics and relaxation. This park has a playground, picnic tables, and a pond with ducks and geese. Visitors can also explore walking trails and enjoy the scenery.

    10. Roswell Symphony Orchestra

    Lastly, a visit to Roswell isn’t complete without experiencing the Roswell Symphony Orchestra. This orchestra has a season of concerts that showcase classical and contemporary music. Visitors can enjoy a night of music and culture.

    Conclusion

    Roswell, New Mexico, has a lot to offer visitors, from natural wonders to art and culture. Using a New Mexico map, you can explore the best places to visit in Roswell in 2023. Don’t miss out on the International UFO Museum and Research Center, Bitter Lake National Wildlife Refuge, Roswell Museum and Art Center, Bottomless Lakes State Park, Anderson Museum of Contemporary Art, Alien Zone, Spring River Park and Zoo, Historical Center for Southeast New Mexico, Cahoon Park, and the Roswell Symphony Orchestra.
