    I-10

    I-10 is the southernmost interstate highway in Texas and stretches from the border with Louisiana to the border with New Mexico. It is the fourth-longest interstate highway in the country, with a total length of 879 miles. I-10 passes through major cities such as Houston, San Antonio, and El Paso.

    I-35

    I-35 is the longest interstate highway in Texas, with a total length of 1,565 miles. It runs from Laredo on the Mexican border to the Red River on the Oklahoma border. I-35 passes through major cities such as San Antonio, Austin, and Dallas.

    I-45

    I-45 is a north-south interstate highway that runs from Galveston on the Gulf of Mexico to Dallas. It has a total length of 285 miles and passes through major cities such as Houston and Conroe.

    Other Interstate Highways

    In addition to the three major interstate highways, Texas has 23 other interstate highways that connect smaller cities and towns throughout the state. Some of these highways include: – I-20, which runs from the border with Louisiana to the border with New Mexico. – I-30, which runs from Fort Worth to Texarkana on the border with Arkansas. – I-40, which runs from the border with New Mexico to the border with Oklahoma. – I-69, which runs from the border with Mexico to the border with Louisiana. – I-410, which is a loop around the city of San Antonio.

    New York City is a bustling metropolis that is home to over 8 million people. The city is divided into five boroughs: Brooklyn, Queens, Manhattan, the Bronx, and Staten Island. Each borough has its unique charm, history, and attractions that make it worth exploring.

    Brooklyn is the most populous borough in New York City, with over 2.5 million residents.     Queens: The Cultural Melting Pot

    Queens is known for its diverse communities and cultural offerings. It is home to the Queens Museum, the New York Hall of Science, and the Queens Botanical Garden. Visitors can also explore the food scene in Queens, which features cuisines from all over the world, including Indian, Chinese, and Greek.

    Manhattan: The Heart of New York City

    Manhattan is the most iconic borough in New York City, with its towering skyscrapers and bustling streets. Visitors can explore famous landmarks, such as the Empire State Building, Central Park, and Times Square. The borough is also home to a plethora of museums, including the Museum of Modern Art and the American Museum of Natural History.

    The Bronx is known for its rich cultural history and contributions to music, particularly hip-hop. Visitors can explore the Bronx Zoo, the New York Botanical Garden, and the Yankee Stadium. The borough is also home to the Grand Concourse, a historic boulevard that features stunning art deco buildings.

    Staten Island: The Greenest Borough

    Staten Island is the least populated borough in New York City, with under 500,000 residents. It is known for its green spaces, including the Greenbelt, a network of parks and trails that spans over 2,800 acres. Visitors can also check out the Staten Island Ferry, which offers stunning views of the Statue of Liberty and the Manhattan skyline.
