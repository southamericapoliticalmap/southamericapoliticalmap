    A total solar eclipse occurs when the moon passes between the earth and the sun, completely blocking the sun’s light for a brief moment. This rare natural phenomenon is a spectacular sight that can only be viewed in certain parts of the world at specific times.

    Best Places to View the Total Solar Eclipse in Indiana

    If you want to witness the total solar eclipse in Indiana, there are several great locations to choose from. Some of the best places to view the eclipse include the Indianapolis Zoo, Mounds State Park, and the Ball State University Observatory.

    What to Expect During the Total Solar Eclipse

    During the total solar eclipse, the sky will become dark and the temperature will drop significantly. Birds may stop singing and animals may become confused. As the eclipse reaches totality, you will be able to see the sun’s corona, which is the outermost layer of its atmosphere.

    Why the Total Solar Eclipse is an Important Scientific Event
