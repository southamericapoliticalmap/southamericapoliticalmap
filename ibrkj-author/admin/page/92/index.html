    The Boundaries of the Republic

    The eastern boundary of the Republic of Texas was the Sabine River, which separated it from Louisiana. The southern boundary was the Rio Grande, which separated it from Mexico. The western boundary was the 103rd meridian, which is now the boundary between Texas and New Mexico. The northern boundary was the Red River, which separated it from Indian Territory, now Oklahoma.

    The Capitals of the Republic

    The Republic of Texas had two capital cities during its brief existence. In 1836, the capital was located in the town of Washington-on-the-Brazos. Later that year, it was moved to Houston, which remained the capital until 1839 when it was moved to Austin.

It represents the struggles and triumphs of the Texan people who fought for their independence. The map also serves as a reminder of the challenges that the republic faced, including the threat of annexation by the United States.
