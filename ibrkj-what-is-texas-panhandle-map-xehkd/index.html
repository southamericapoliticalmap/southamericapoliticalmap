    History of the Texas Panhandle

    The Panhandle region of Texas was originally inhabited by various Native American tribes, including the Comanche, Apache, and Kiowa. The region was later claimed by Spain, Mexico, and finally the Republic of Texas before becoming part of the United States in 1845. The Panhandle was initially used for ranching and farming, and it continues to be an important agricultural region to this day.

    The Boundaries of the Texas Panhandle

    The Texas Panhandle is bordered by Oklahoma to the north, New Mexico to the west, and the rest of Texas to the east and south. The region covers approximately 26,000 square miles and includes 26 counties. The major cities in the Panhandle include Amarillo, Borger, Pampa, and Dumas.
